    Course Description
    🔗
    An examination of the language of visual communication, focusing on the principles of design. Students explore line, shape, form, value, texture, color, and space in relation to various compositional strategies. Lecture and discussion sessions introduce historical and contemporary theories of two-dimensional design.

    Subsection NACE Core Competencies

    Ask employers what they want from new graduates, and communication skills top the list. The NACE competencies spotlight the tools current students need to find work in an ever changing job market. Color and Design asks students to consistently practice the following competencies:
    🔗
    • Communication - Understanding the importance of and demonstrate verbal, written, and non-verbal/body language, abilities. Employing active listening, persuasion, and influencing skills.
    🔗
    • Self development- Showing an awareness of own strengths and areas for development. Identify areas for continual growth while pursuing and applying feedback.
    🔗
    • Critical thinking - Multi-tasking in a fast-paced environment.
    🔗
    • Professionalism - Maintaining a positive personal brand in alignment with organization and personal career values. Being present and prepared. Demonstrating dependability (e.g., report consistently for work or meetings). Prioritizing and completing tasks to accomplish organizational goals.

    Subsection Grading

    You will be graded based on your effort and progress over the time of the course. You will receive a grade for each project and assignment. Any project can be reworked and turned in later in the semester for an improved grade.
    🔗
    • 35%: participation - attendance, class discussion, working during class time, initiative, communication with professor and peers
    🔗
    • 35%: process - depth of exploration, concepts, experimentation
    🔗
    • 30%: presentation - quality of final projects and assignments

    Subsection Attendance

    Please attend class. This is a studio class. Class begins promptly at 8:30 AM. Arriving late or leaving early is disruptive and will affect your grade. If you have an emergency or you are sick, please email me before the beginning of class. If I don’t hear from you, you will not receive instructions.
    🔗
    • 4 absences before mid semester will result in a failing grade for the course • 4 absences over the semester will result in one full grade drop from your FINAL course grade (for example: from a ‘B’ to a ‘C’). • 6 absences will result in a failing grade for the course
    🔗
    The information in this syllabus is subject to change. Students will be notified of changes.

    Subsection Framingham State Policies and Services

    Academic Services The Center for Academic Success and Achievement, or CASA, offers professional and peer tutoring, academic success coaching, and disability resources and accommodations. CASA is also home to GenerationOne, an affinity group for first-generation college students as well as community outreach and mentor-ship programs. CASA is open to all current students.
    🔗
    Academic Misconduct All students in attendance at Framingham State University are expected to be honorable and to observe standards of conduct appropriate to a community of scholars. The Institution expects from its students a higher standard of conduct than the minimum required to avoid discipline. Academic misconduct includes all acts of dishonesty in any academically related matter and any knowing or intentional help or attempt to help, or conspiracy to help, another student. The university policy regarding academic honesty will be followed in the event of academic misconduct. (*See Framingham State University Undergraduate Catalog).
    🔗
    Security/Safety/Responsibility The computers are for students enrolled in graphic design courses. Printing in the computer lab is for graphic design only (for example, please do not print a 150 page history book PDF). Please do not be careless with or waste paper and ink in the lab. Framingham State University and the Department of Art and Music are not responsible for student work and/or personal belongings left unsecured in classrooms. Lockers and storage facilities are available for use during the semester for students enrolled in this course. Please be aware that open shelving is used at your own risk. Students should be particularly mindful of leaving new material out at the beginning and end of the semester.
